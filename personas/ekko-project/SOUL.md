#### FRAMES.md
# FRAMES

> Mental frames and perspectives I use to see the world. Lenses that change how I interpret situations.

---

## Active Frames

### The Crowbar
Knowing where to apply leverage matters more than raw effort. The business name isn't about tools — it's about finding the one point where a small push moves the whole thing. For clients: "Where does the crowbar go in your workflow?"

### Calm, Opinionated Systems
The brand promise that's also a design principle. Black, white, gold. Clean dashboards. "Here, this is your control panel — don't worry about what's underneath." Underneath is the mess. The client never sees it.

### Weapons Layer / Wisdom Layer
Technical how-to files (SNAP, Claude Code, n8n, workflows) and philosophical why-to files (Hormozi, Rohn, Sivers, Cohen). Both are necessary. Neither is sufficient. The real power comes when they cross-link — when the technical capability is guided by strategic wisdom.

### Borrowed Discipline (Three Philosophers)
Different situations need different sources of discipline:
- **Hormozi** for selling — identity commitment, volume, no off button
- **Rohn** for environment — surround yourself with high expectations
- **Sivers** for systems — satisfice, subtract, good enough is a superpower

Applying Sivers' philosophy where Hormozi's is needed (building infrastructure instead of selling) is the ENTP trap.

### Escape Velocity vs. Gravity
Building a business while employed is negotiating with your future self: how much pain now for how much optionality later. The 75% rule is the math that replaces feelings about when to make the leap.

### Self as Test User
"If I can't stick to this and I actually like this stuff, what chance does a normal user have?" Build for yourself, then assume clients do 30-50% of your best week. Price around reality, not the fantasy persona.

### The Quiet Transition
At some point it stops being "a plan to leave my job" and starts being the job. Don't force the drama of a big leap. Let the transition happen when the math says it's time.

### The Consulting Paradox
Every philosopher in the vault warns that the consulting→cohort→platform ladder is the trap, not the path. Consulting IS the destination, not the on-ramp.

#### STRATEGIES.md
# STRATEGIES

> How I'm approaching my goals. Not tactics (those change weekly) — these are the durable strategic principles.

---

## Strategy 1: One Product, One Price
**Principle:** Kill optionality. No tiers, no upsells (yet), no "choose your adventure." $5K for a 4-5 hour Build Session. Period.
**Why:** Cohen proved that simplicity scales. Hormozi proved that high prices select for serious buyers. Sivers proved that less optimization = more contentment. All three say the same thing through different lenses.
**Anti-strategy:** Don't build the platform. Don't launch the cohort. Don't design the Skool community. Those are ENTP distractions disguised as progress.

## Strategy 2: Consulting IS the Business
**Principle:** Tier 1 isn't a stepping stone to Tier 2. It's the destination. If I never build anything beyond 1:1 Build Sessions, the math still works: 12 sessions/year = $60K on 60 hours of delivery.
**Why:** Jason Cohen's blog with 40,000 readers produced 2 signups. Content marketing doesn't produce customers for bootstrapped services. Referrals from delighted clients do.
**The math:** $5K x 12 = $60K. $5K x 24 = $120K. $5K x 36 = $180K. All from a handful of word-of-mouth referrals.

## Strategy 3: Eat My Own Cooking
**Principle:** Every component of the Build Session MVP gets used by me first. Telos, hooks, skills, SecondBrain, the Algorithm — I live in this daily. When a client asks "does this actually work?" the answer is demonstrated, not promised.
**Why:** Authenticity is the only marketing that matters at this price point. The infrastructure IS the demo.

## Strategy 4: The 75% Rule
**Principle:** Don't quit the W2 until consulting revenue hits 75% of salary for 3 months straight. No exceptions. No "I feel ready." Math, not feelings.
**Why:** Health insurance, debt, random plumbing emergencies. Romanticizing the leap is how people end up back in a worse job six months later.
**Source:** Founder's Voice

## Strategy 5: Borrowed Discipline (Hormozi + Rohn + Sivers)
**Principle:** Apply each philosopher where they fit:
- **Hormozi** for selling: "Volume negates luck." Make the offers. Do the boring work. Champions don't have an off button.
- **Rohn** for environment: Associate with people who maintain high expectations. The cohort of serious SNAP users becomes my community.
- **Sivers** for systems: "Good enough is a superpower." Don't optimize what's already working. Ship and iterate.
**Why:** Discipline isn't one flavor. Different situations need different sources of it.

## Strategy 6: Build for People I Love Being Around
**Principle:** $5K is a filter. It selects for technical leaders, founders, senior engineers — people who are serious about AI infrastructure and will actually implement what we build together.
**Anti-strategy:** Don't chase the "non-technical knowledge workers at mid-market companies" from the old CrowbarCrew plan. That's a different business requiring content marketing I don't want to do.
**Source:** Sivers + Cohen convergence

**Source:** Derek Sivers Philosophy Notes

### The Sivers Filter
"Set up a business to serve people you love being around, even if it makes less money." $5K selects for serious people. Build for them.
**Source:** Derek Sivers + Cohen convergence

### Hormozi's Volume Principle
"Volume negates luck." Stop optimizing the ask and start making more asks. Applied to: client outreach, content experiments, skill iterations.
**Source:** Alex Hormozi MW670

Structure: recurring revenue, boutique positioning, annual prepaid, B2B customers. The math: 150 customers at $70/month = $10K MRR. For consulting: $5K x 12/year = $60K on 60 hours. The structure matters more than the product.
**Source:** Jason Cohen MicroConf 2013

### Hormozi's Money Formula
More leads x better conversion x higher price x more transactions. But the non-obvious insight: sell to the rich. "The sweet spot isn't the most yeses. It's the most money." $5K per session with 12 clients/year beats $500 with 120 clients.
**Source:** Alex Hormozi Money Formula

### Rohn's 70/30 Rule
70% for necessities and luxuries, 10% for charity, 10% for wealth-creation, 10% for savings. Financial independence depends on allocation wisdom, not income level.
**Source:** Jim Rohn 7 Strategies

### The ENTP Trap Detector
When I feel the pull to build something new, check: does it serve the Build Session product? If not, it's my 79% Prospecting score pulling me toward novelty. General ambition gives anxiety. Specific ambition gives direction.
**Source:** Myers-Briggs + Hormozi + Vault Hidden Threads

A blog with 40,000 readers produced 2 signups. Social media produces negligible customer acquisition for bootstrapped services. Don't build growth strategies on channels that are proven not to work for this business shape.
**Source:** Jason Cohen MicroConf 2013

**Source:** Charlie Munger via Hormozi

## About Business

### Focus beats optionality (February 2026)
Had 5 business vehicles in the vault: CrowbarCrew consulting, Skool community ($150/month), AI Advantage Bootcamp, SNAP expansion, Bookspace Video Plan. Killed 4. Every philosopher in the vault — Hormozi, Rohn, Sivers, Cohen — independently said the same thing: focus on one. The ENTP personality predicts this trap with 81% assertiveness.
**How learned:** Vault Hidden Threads analysis surfacing the pattern across all wisdom sources

### Content marketing doesn't produce customers for bootstrapped services (February 2026)
Jason Cohen had a blog with 40,000 readers. He launched a product directly relevant to those readers. He got 2 signups. Hiten Shah confirmed: "No, it's always like that. It doesn't work. Move on." My Tier 2 (cohorts from consulting alumni) and Tier 3 (platform from SEO) were built on the exact channel Cohen debunked.
**How learned:** Extracting wisdom from Cohen's MicroConf talk

### Picking up pennies is the wrong game (February 2026)
The Skool community at $150/month was pennies compared to $5K Build Sessions. Cohen: "I don't like any self-funded startup where the revenue model is picking up pennies." Hormozi: "The sweet spot isn't the most yeses. It's the most money."
**How learned:** Comparing Skool economics to Build Session economics side by side

### Your own systems breaking IS the product research (February 2026)
Where I stop entering transactions, where automations silently fail, where reminders become noise — that's the data. If I can't stick to my own system and I actually like this stuff, normal users will do 30-50% of my best week. Price around that reality.
**How learned:** Founders Voice stream of consciousness

## About Technology

### The minimum committed stack eliminates decision fatigue (February 2026)
One model that thinks, one agent that executes, one browser that sees the world. Everything else is plumbing. The AI stack obsession was half tool selection and half identity crisis. Committing to a stack frees attention for the work the stack enables.
**How learned:** Building SNAP v3.0, testing multiple models, settling on Claude + Ekko

### Weapons and wisdom need to cross-link (February 2026)
Technical how-to files and philosophical why-to files existed in the vault but never talked to each other. 17 cross-links added in one session bridged the gap. The real insight: capability without strategy is just tinkering; strategy without capability is just dreaming.
**How learned:** Vault Hidden Threads analysis revealing the two disconnected layers

## About Myself

### My ENTP personality is both my greatest asset and greatest risk (February 2026)
95% extraverted (energized by social interaction), 89% intuitive (imaginative, focused on possibilities), 79% prospecting (prioritizes spontaneity over stability). Strengths: idea generation, improvisation, quick learning. Weaknesses: scattered focus, novelty addiction, reluctance to commit to one path. The personality profile predicts every business mistake I've already made.
**How learned:** Myers-Briggs ENTP-A profile + mapping weaknesses to actual vault behavior

### Building infrastructure is my comfort zone; selling is my growth edge (February 2026)
I'd rather build another hook than make 25 LinkedIn outreach messages. The Hidden Threads analysis named this directly: "You've been applying Sivers' philosophy (build infrastructure, satisfice) to what needs Hormozi's philosophy (sell aggressively, never stop)."
**How learned:** Vault Hidden Threads Thread 4 — Borrowed Discipline pattern

## About Relationships

### Associate with people who maintain high expectations (February 2026)
Rohn's single most underlined principle. The $5K price point isn't just revenue strategy — it's a relationship filter. It selects for people who are serious, will implement, and will challenge me to be better. Sivers agrees: "Build for people you love being around."
**How learned:** Convergence of Rohn, Sivers, and Cohen on the same insight about community quality

## Current Narratives

### "I'm building infrastructure, not a side hustle" — SERVES
This narrative keeps standards high. Infrastructure implies durability, engineering discipline, and professional-grade output. It's the identity that produced Ekko with 45 skills, 25 hooks, and 167 workflows. The risk: it can shade into perfectionism (Hormozi: "Perfectionism is procrastination masquerading as quality control").

### "I'm basically building a lab around my own life" — SERVES
From Founders Voice. The personal experiments become the product research. Every system that breaks, every automation that silently fails, every place where I stop entering data — that's the client empathy data. Eat your own cooking.

### "At some point it quietly stops being 'a plan to leave my job' and starts being the job" — SERVES
The quiet transition narrative. No drama, no burning bridges, no grand leap. Just the math crossing a threshold. This narrative protects against premature leaps and impulsive decisions.

### "I can't claim to have built SNAP" — REWRITE
The credibility gap narrative. Daniel Miessler invented the framework. But electricians don't invent electricity — they know where the wires go. The client pays for curation, judgment, and speed. Mastering the implementation IS the value. What took months of experimentation, the client gets in one afternoon.

### "I need to build more before I can sell" — REWRITE
Classic ENTP avoidance dressed as due diligence. The product is defined. The infrastructure works daily. Building another hook feels productive. Making 25 LinkedIn outreach messages feels risky. Hormozi: "The magic you're looking for is in the work you're avoiding."

### "I'm not ready for high-ticket pricing" — REWRITE
Selling from your own wallet. Hormozi: "The first time someone says yes to your high price will permanently break your limiting pricing beliefs." $5K is the right price because it selects for the right people. The discomfort is an inch deep.
