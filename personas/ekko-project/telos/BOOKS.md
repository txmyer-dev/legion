# BOOKS

> Books that shaped my thinking. Not a reading list — a hall of influences.

---

## Transformative

### "7 Strategies for Wealth & Happiness" — Jim Rohn
The 70/30 financial allocation rule. "What you become is far more important than what you get." "Success leaves clues." The book that made personal development feel like engineering, not self-help. Two full wisdom extractions in SecondBrain.
**Impact:** Financial framework, association principle, single-goal focus

### "How to Have the Best Year Ever!" — Jim Rohn
The companion seminar/book. Vision + goals + action + discipline + learning + community as integrated system. "Consistency beats intensity" as a core principle. Four hours of material distilled into habits I reference weekly.
**Impact:** Environmental design, habit replacement, mortality awareness as motivator

## Useful

### "The Paradox of Choice" — Barry Schwartz
Referenced by Derek Sivers. Satisficers vs. maximizers — satisficers feel better about decisions despite not always making the optimal choice. "Good enough is a superpower" is the one-line summary. Applied to stack selection, infrastructure decisions, and feature completeness.
**Impact:** Permission to stop optimizing, satisficing as deliberate strategy

### "The Moral Landscape" — Sam Harris
Referenced by Derek Sivers. Judge morally based on individual well-being. The tension between unorthodox life choices and consistent moral compass. Used more as a lens than a rulebook.
**Impact:** Personal moral framework beyond inherited rules

### "$100M Offers" / "$100M Leads" — Alex Hormozi (implicit)
Not explicitly named in extractions but the framework behind 186 quotes across 7 episodes. Volume negates luck. Sell to the rich. Pricing as two-way communication. The money formula: more leads x better conversion x higher price x more transactions.
**Impact:** Pricing confidence, selling philosophy, volume principle

### "Useful Not True" — Derek Sivers
The philosophy that beliefs are placebos chosen to serve a purpose. Choose empowering beliefs deliberately. Applied to: the credibility gap narrative, pricing confidence, ENTP pattern rewriting.
**Impact:** Belief as tool, not truth

## On Deck

### "Anything You Want" — Derek Sivers
Referenced in Philosophy Notes. Short manifesto on building a business you actually want. Likely reinforces the Sivers filter ("build for people you love being around").

### "Can't Hurt Me" — David Goggins
Referenced via Hormozi: "It is so easy to be successful nowadays because people are weak." The identity-as-discipline angle that complements Hormozi's "champions don't have an off button."
