## Active Challenges

### Balancing W2 Demands with Consulting Build-Out
Working full-time while building a consulting practice. Sprint retros by day, Build Session prep by night. The 75% rule means the W2 stays until $7.5K/month consulting revenue sustains for 3 months. This is a condition to navigate, not a problem to eliminate — the W2 provides stability, health insurance, and forces discipline.

### Time Scarcity for Client Acquisition
10-20 hours per week is the realistic window. Can't do extensive marketing while employed full-time. This means the client acquisition strategy MUST be high-leverage: warm network, referrals, not content marketing or cold outreach at scale. Every hour spent must count.

### Selling Discomfort as Growth Edge
Infrastructure is the comfort zone. Selling is where growth happens. The ENTP personality (79% Prospecting, 95% Extraverted) loves building and improvising but resists the structured, repetitive work of outreach. The challenge isn't learning to sell — it's learning to tolerate the discomfort of selling regularly.

### First Client Validation Gap
The product is defined but unvalidated by a paying customer. First client proves or disproves: the pricing, the 4-5 hour session format, the hour-by-hour plan, and whether the deliverable (a working SNAP) actually transfers. Everything before the first client is theory.

### ENTP Novelty Addiction
The personality profile predicts scattered focus with 79% prospecting score. The urge to build new things instead of selling existing ones is a permanent condition, not a one-time problem. Mitigation is structural: single product, single price, and the checkpoint question "does this serve the Build Session?"
