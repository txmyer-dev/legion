# FRAMES

> Mental frames and perspectives I use to see the world. Lenses that change how I interpret situations.

---

## Active Frames

### The Crowbar
Knowing where to apply leverage matters more than raw effort. The business name isn't about tools — it's about finding the one point where a small push moves the whole thing. For clients: "Where does the crowbar go in your workflow?"

### Calm, Opinionated Systems
The brand promise that's also a design principle. Black, white, gold. Clean dashboards. "Here, this is your control panel — don't worry about what's underneath." Underneath is the mess. The client never sees it.

### Weapons Layer / Wisdom Layer
Technical how-to files (SNAP, Claude Code, n8n, workflows) and philosophical why-to files (Hormozi, Rohn, Sivers, Cohen). Both are necessary. Neither is sufficient. The real power comes when they cross-link — when the technical capability is guided by strategic wisdom.

### Borrowed Discipline (Three Philosophers)
Different situations need different sources of discipline:
- **Hormozi** for selling — identity commitment, volume, no off button
- **Rohn** for environment — surround yourself with high expectations
- **Sivers** for systems — satisfice, subtract, good enough is a superpower

Applying Sivers' philosophy where Hormozi's is needed (building infrastructure instead of selling) is the ENTP trap.

### Escape Velocity vs. Gravity
Building a business while employed is negotiating with your future self: how much pain now for how much optionality later. The 75% rule is the math that replaces feelings about when to make the leap.

### Self as Test User
"If I can't stick to this and I actually like this stuff, what chance does a normal user have?" Build for yourself, then assume clients do 30-50% of your best week. Price around reality, not the fantasy persona.

### The Quiet Transition
At some point it stops being "a plan to leave my job" and starts being the job. Don't force the drama of a big leap. Let the transition happen when the math says it's time.

### The Consulting Paradox
Every philosopher in the vault warns that the consulting→cohort→platform ladder is the trap, not the path. Consulting IS the destination, not the on-ramp.
