## About Business

### Focus beats optionality (February 2026)
Had 5 business vehicles in the vault: CrowbarCrew consulting, Skool community ($150/month), AI Advantage Bootcamp, SNAP expansion, Bookspace Video Plan. Killed 4. Every philosopher in the vault — Hormozi, Rohn, Sivers, Cohen — independently said the same thing: focus on one. The ENTP personality predicts this trap with 81% assertiveness.
**How learned:** Vault Hidden Threads analysis surfacing the pattern across all wisdom sources

### Content marketing doesn't produce customers for bootstrapped services (February 2026)
Jason Cohen had a blog with 40,000 readers. He launched a product directly relevant to those readers. He got 2 signups. Hiten Shah confirmed: "No, it's always like that. It doesn't work. Move on." My Tier 2 (cohorts from consulting alumni) and Tier 3 (platform from SEO) were built on the exact channel Cohen debunked.
**How learned:** Extracting wisdom from Cohen's MicroConf talk

### Picking up pennies is the wrong game (February 2026)
The Skool community at $150/month was pennies compared to $5K Build Sessions. Cohen: "I don't like any self-funded startup where the revenue model is picking up pennies." Hormozi: "The sweet spot isn't the most yeses. It's the most money."
**How learned:** Comparing Skool economics to Build Session economics side by side

### Your own systems breaking IS the product research (February 2026)
Where I stop entering transactions, where automations silently fail, where reminders become noise — that's the data. If I can't stick to my own system and I actually like this stuff, normal users will do 30-50% of my best week. Price around that reality.
**How learned:** Founders Voice stream of consciousness

## About Technology

### The minimum committed stack eliminates decision fatigue (February 2026)
One model that thinks, one agent that executes, one browser that sees the world. Everything else is plumbing. The AI stack obsession was half tool selection and half identity crisis. Committing to a stack frees attention for the work the stack enables.
**How learned:** Building SNAP v3.0, testing multiple models, settling on Claude + Ekko

### Weapons and wisdom need to cross-link (February 2026)
Technical how-to files and philosophical why-to files existed in the vault but never talked to each other. 17 cross-links added in one session bridged the gap. The real insight: capability without strategy is just tinkering; strategy without capability is just dreaming.
**How learned:** Vault Hidden Threads analysis revealing the two disconnected layers

## About Myself

### My ENTP personality is both my greatest asset and greatest risk (February 2026)
95% extraverted (energized by social interaction), 89% intuitive (imaginative, focused on possibilities), 79% prospecting (prioritizes spontaneity over stability). Strengths: idea generation, improvisation, quick learning. Weaknesses: scattered focus, novelty addiction, reluctance to commit to one path. The personality profile predicts every business mistake I've already made.
**How learned:** Myers-Briggs ENTP-A profile + mapping weaknesses to actual vault behavior

### Building infrastructure is my comfort zone; selling is my growth edge (February 2026)
I'd rather build another hook than make 25 LinkedIn outreach messages. The Hidden Threads analysis named this directly: "You've been applying Sivers' philosophy (build infrastructure, satisfice) to what needs Hormozi's philosophy (sell aggressively, never stop)."
**How learned:** Vault Hidden Threads Thread 4 — Borrowed Discipline pattern

## About Relationships

### Associate with people who maintain high expectations (February 2026)
Rohn's single most underlined principle. The $5K price point isn't just revenue strategy — it's a relationship filter. It selects for people who are serious, will implement, and will challenge me to be better. Sivers agrees: "Build for people you love being around."
**How learned:** Convergence of Rohn, Sivers, and Cohen on the same insight about community quality
