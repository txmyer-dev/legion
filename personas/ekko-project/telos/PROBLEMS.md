# PROBLEMS

> Problems I'm actively working to solve. Not complaints — strategic obstacles that need clearing.

---

## The ENTP Focus Problem
**Severity:** High
**Description:** My personality type (ENTP-A, 79% Prospecting) predicts I'll keep building infrastructure instead of selling. I had 5 business vehicles in my vault before the pivot. Hormozi, Rohn, Sivers, and Cohen all independently say the same thing: focus on one.
**Current mitigation:** Archived 4 of 5 vehicles. Single product, single price. Every time I feel the pull to build a new thing, check whether it serves the Build Session product.
**Source:** Myers-Briggs profile + Vault Hidden Threads analysis

## The Credibility Gap
**Severity:** Medium
**Description:** I feel like I can't claim to have "built SNAP" when most of what I've done is customize on top of Daniel Miessler's work. This creates hesitation in selling.
**Reframe:** I didn't invent the framework — I mastered the implementation. That's what the client is paying for: curation, judgment, and speed. Electricians don't invent electricity. They know where the wires go.
**Source:** Founder's Voice + SNAP Build Session product definition

## Finding the First Client
**Severity:** High
**Description:** The product is defined but no one has paid for it yet. First client validates everything — pricing, timing, deliverables, the hour-by-hour plan.
**Approach:** Warm network first. Free or discounted first session to build the case study. Then referral-driven growth.
**Constraint:** Can't do extensive marketing while working the W2. The 75% rule means I need the W2 stable until consulting revenue proves itself.

## The Selling Discomfort
**Severity:** Medium
**Description:** Infrastructure is my comfort zone. Selling is my growth edge. I'd rather build another hook than make 25 LinkedIn outreach messages.
**Insight from vault:** "The first time someone says yes to your high price will permanently break your limiting pricing beliefs." — Hormozi. The discomfort is an inch deep. Push through it once.

## Time Constraint
**Severity:** Medium
**Description:** Full-time W2 means Build Sessions need to happen evenings or weekends. Target clients also have day jobs. Need to find the scheduling pattern that works.
**Approach:** Weekend sessions (Saturday mornings) or after-hours blocks. $5K justifies flexible scheduling.
