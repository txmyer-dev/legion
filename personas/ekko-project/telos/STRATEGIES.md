# STRATEGIES

> How I'm approaching my goals. Not tactics (those change weekly) — these are the durable strategic principles.

---

## Strategy 1: One Product, One Price
**Principle:** Kill optionality. No tiers, no upsells (yet), no "choose your adventure." $5K for a 4-5 hour Build Session. Period.
**Why:** Cohen proved that simplicity scales. Hormozi proved that high prices select for serious buyers. Sivers proved that less optimization = more contentment. All three say the same thing through different lenses.
**Anti-strategy:** Don't build the platform. Don't launch the cohort. Don't design the Skool community. Those are ENTP distractions disguised as progress.

## Strategy 2: Consulting IS the Business
**Principle:** Tier 1 isn't a stepping stone to Tier 2. It's the destination. If I never build anything beyond 1:1 Build Sessions, the math still works: 12 sessions/year = $60K on 60 hours of delivery.
**Why:** Jason Cohen's blog with 40,000 readers produced 2 signups. Content marketing doesn't produce customers for bootstrapped services. Referrals from delighted clients do.
**The math:** $5K x 12 = $60K. $5K x 24 = $120K. $5K x 36 = $180K. All from a handful of word-of-mouth referrals.

## Strategy 3: Eat My Own Cooking
**Principle:** Every component of the Build Session MVP gets used by me first. Telos, hooks, skills, SecondBrain, the Algorithm — I live in this daily. When a client asks "does this actually work?" the answer is demonstrated, not promised.
**Why:** Authenticity is the only marketing that matters at this price point. The infrastructure IS the demo.

## Strategy 4: The 75% Rule
**Principle:** Don't quit the W2 until consulting revenue hits 75% of salary for 3 months straight. No exceptions. No "I feel ready." Math, not feelings.
**Why:** Health insurance, debt, random plumbing emergencies. Romanticizing the leap is how people end up back in a worse job six months later.
**Source:** Founder's Voice

## Strategy 5: Borrowed Discipline (Hormozi + Rohn + Sivers)
**Principle:** Apply each philosopher where they fit:
- **Hormozi** for selling: "Volume negates luck." Make the offers. Do the boring work. Champions don't have an off button.
- **Rohn** for environment: Associate with people who maintain high expectations. The cohort of serious SNAP users becomes my community.
- **Sivers** for systems: "Good enough is a superpower." Don't optimize what's already working. Ship and iterate.
**Why:** Discipline isn't one flavor. Different situations need different sources of it.

## Strategy 6: Build for People I Love Being Around
**Principle:** $5K is a filter. It selects for technical leaders, founders, senior engineers — people who are serious about AI infrastructure and will actually implement what we build together.
**Anti-strategy:** Don't chase the "non-technical knowledge workers at mid-market companies" from the old CrowbarCrew plan. That's a different business requiring content marketing I don't want to do.
**Source:** Sivers + Cohen convergence
