# WISDOM

> Accumulated wisdom from mentors, books, and hard experience. Things I've internalized, not just heard.

---

## From Alex Hormozi

- "Volume negates luck." — Do the boring work. Make the offers. The bar for winning has never been so low.
- "Success comes down to doing the obvious thing for an extraordinary period of time without convincing yourself you're smarter than you are."
- "You don't feel confident by shouting affirmations in the mirror but by having an undeniable stack of proof that you are who you say you are."
- "Sell to the rich. They pay better. It's not some piffy statement. It's reality."
- "Pricing is a two-way communication. You tell them what you're about, and then they will self-select."
- "The biggest risk to your future isn't your competition — it's the distractions you insist on keeping in your life."
- "General ambition gives you anxiety; specific ambition gives you direction."
- "Businesses die of indigestion, not starvation." — Andy Grove via Hormozi
- "The magic you're looking for is in the work you're avoiding."
- "Stop selling from your own wallet."
- "The first time someone says yes to your high price will permanently break your limiting pricing beliefs."
- "Do not underestimate the power of large prices in small quantities."
- "Victims see their past as their fate. Champions see their past as their origin story."

## From Jim Rohn

- "What you become is far more important than what you get." — Personal transformation precedes external success.
- "Success leaves clues." — Study successful people's habits, tactics, and strategies. They're observable and replicable.
- "All leaders are readers." — Reading discipline separates leaders from followers.
- "The influence of those around us is so powerful, so subtle, so gradual that often we don't even realize how it can affect us."
- "Be happy with what you have while pursuing what you want."
- "To have more than you've got, you have to become more than you are."
- The 70/30 Rule: 70% for necessities and luxuries, 10% for charity, 10% for wealth-creation, 10% for savings.
- Single annual goal focus amplifies business growth dramatically compared to scattered attention across competing objectives.

## From Derek Sivers

- "Good enough is a superpower." — Stop maximizing everything. Satisficers feel better about decisions than maximizers.
- "Set up a business to serve people you love being around, even if it makes less money." — That's the filter.
- "Useful Not True" — Beliefs are placebos. Choose beliefs that serve your goals, not "truth."
- Mentor yourself by thinking from another person's perspective. "What would [person I admire] do?"
- Labels and identities are temporary and subject to change — don't get too attached.
- Tech independence: own your data, own your server, own your domain. Start small, build confidence.
- Success = achieving personal goals without needing external validation. Once you win the game, stop playing and move on.

## From Jason Cohen

- A self-funded startup should function as a predictable cash machine generating reliable monthly profit.
- You only need 150 customers paying $70/month to reach $10K MRR. Small numbers, high value.
- "Boutique" reframes small and expensive as something customers feel good about supporting.
- Social media marketing produces negligible customer acquisition. His blog with 40,000 readers produced 2 signups.
- Raise prices until something bad happens. Most founders dramatically undercharge.
- "The hardest thing about building a successful self-funded company is knowing yourself and what you actually want."
- "I don't like any self-funded startup where the revenue model is picking up pennies."
- Replace free trials with money-back guarantees — collect revenue immediately while reducing risk.

## From Experience

- If I can't stick to my own systems and I actually like this stuff, a normal client will do 30-50% of my best week. Price around that reality.
- Five business vehicles in one vault is the ENTP trap. The vault already contains every answer — the problem was never missing knowledge, it was scattered focus.
- The weapons layer (technical how-to) and wisdom layer (philosophical why-to) need to talk to each other. Both matter. Neither is sufficient alone.
- Building infrastructure feels productive but can be avoidance of selling. Infrastructure is my comfort zone. Selling is my growth edge.
