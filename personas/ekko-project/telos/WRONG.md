# WRONG

> Things I was wrong about. Growth tracking. The ability to change your mind is a superpower.

## Changed My Mind On

### "I need a tiered business model to scale"
**Believed:** A consulting→cohort→platform ladder is how bootstrapped businesses grow. Tier 1 is the on-ramp, Tier 2 and 3 are the real business.
**Now believe:** Consulting IS the business. The ladder was the trap. $5K x 12 sessions = $60K on 60 hours delivery. 95% margin. No platform needed.
**What changed my mind:** Jason Cohen proved content marketing fails for bootstrapped acquisition (40K readers → 2 signups). Vault Hidden Threads showed all four philosophers converge on the same answer: Tier 1 alone works.

### "A $150/month Skool community is viable"
**Believed:** A premium Skool community could generate recurring revenue from SNAP consulting alumni.
**Now believe:** $150/month is picking up pennies. Cohen: "I don't like any self-funded startup where the revenue model is picking up pennies." One $5K session = 33 months of one Skool subscriber.
**What changed my mind:** Side-by-side economics comparison. Hormozi: "The sweet spot isn't the most yeses. It's the most money."

### "Content marketing will build my customer funnel"
**Believed:** Blog posts, social content, and SEO would drive leads into the consulting pipeline.
**Now believe:** Social media marketing produces negligible customer acquisition for bootstrapped services. The channel is referrals from delighted clients.
**What changed my mind:** Cohen's hard data: a blog with 40,000 readers produced 2 signups. Hiten Shah confirmed: "It's always like that. It doesn't work. Move on."

### "I need multiple business ideas to find the one that works"
**Believed:** Having 5 business vehicles (CrowbarCrew, Skool, Bootcamp, SNAP expansion, Bookspace) was smart diversification.
**Now believe:** It was ENTP-driven novelty addiction disguised as strategy. Hormozi: "General ambition gives you anxiety; specific ambition gives you direction." Rohn: single annual goal focus amplifies growth.
**What changed my mind:** Myers-Briggs profile predicting this exact behavior + Hidden Threads analysis naming the pattern.

### "I should target non-technical knowledge workers"
**Believed:** The CrowbarCrew plan targeted "non-technical knowledge workers at mid-market companies" — the "Excel-to-AI bridge" crowd.
**Now believe:** The gap between who I build SNAP for (deeply technical power user) and that target market is enormous. The right clients are CTOs, technical founders, and senior engineers who want their own Ekko.
**What changed my mind:** Hidden Threads Thread 3 — "SNAP Is The Product, You Just Haven't Named It." Hormozi: "Sell to the rich." Cohen: "B2B markets are fundamentally more conducive to sustainable self-funded startups."
